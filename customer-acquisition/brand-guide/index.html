01 — Color System

A palette that makes an argument
before a word is read

Every color was chosen to occupy space no real estate competitor currently owns — and to trigger the right psychological response in each ICP segment.

#0D1B2A Primary BG
Slate Midnight
Backgrounds · Navigation · App UI
Not sterile white, not stale navy — the dark-mode native aesthetic of modern fintech (Robinhood, Mercury, Brex). Signals authority and sophistication without the musty formality of traditional real estate.
Primary Background
#00C47A Primary Brand
Forest Signal
Logo · CTAs · Key data · Savings highlights
Green subconsciously signals wealth, growth, and "go." Energetic enough to signal disruption — mature enough to signal trust. Users process it as "good" before reading a single word. No real estate competitor owns this space.
Primary Brand Color
#00D4FF AI / Tech
Electric Teal
AI features · Chatbot UI · Technology labels
Signals innovation, forward-thinking intelligence — exclusively applied to AI-powered features. This creates a visual language: when you see teal, you know AI is doing the work. Differentiates the AI layer from financial trust (green).
AI Feature Accent
#F4F7F5 Light BG
Off-White Clarity
Section backgrounds · Cards · Content areas
Slightly warm to avoid the clinical coldness of pure white — which would make the platform feel like every other proptech competitor. Communicates openness, transparency, and the absence of hidden complexity. The opposite of the paperwork nightmare.
Light Backgrounds
#6B7A8D Body Text
Slate Gray
Body copy · Labels · Secondary info
Balanced and approachable — not so dark it feels heavy, not so light it becomes illegible. Provides breathing room between primary content and background, keeping the eye focused on what matters: the savings numbers and CTAs.
Supporting Text
#F5C842 Highlights
Savings Gold
Dollar savings amounts · Calculator results · Transaction wins
Used sparingly and exclusively for financial reward moments — when you see gold, you just saved money. Never used as a primary brand color (too legacy real estate). On dark backgrounds it creates an unmissable focal point: "this is the number that matters."

Colors to avoid — and why

These are not arbitrary — each one would cause a specific psychological misfire with the ICP.

Red
In financial contexts, red = losses. Also owned by RE/MAX, KW — signals "establishment real estate."
Navy Blue
Old-guard banking and traditional real estate (Zillow, Redfin). Communicates establishment, not disruption.
Purple
Increasingly signals "crypto/blockchain" — a trust-ambiguous association in a high-stakes transaction context.
Bright Orange
Can feel frivolous for a $430K transaction. Requires careful execution to maintain trust in financial categories.
Gold (as primary)
Too Century 21 — signals "old money establishment," the exact opposite of the disruptor positioning.
Pure White BG
Clinical and indistinguishable from every Zillow/Redfin/Opendoor clone. The off-white warmth is the differentiator.

Why Manrope over the alternatives

vs. Inter
Inter is ubiquitous — every SaaS company uses it. Manrope has more personality while retaining the same functional excellence. It has visual presence at display sizes that Inter lacks.
vs. DM Sans
DM Sans is playful — excellent for consumer apps but can undermine credibility for a $400,000 transaction. Manrope threads the needle between DM Sans's friendliness and Inter's precision.
vs. Montserrat
Montserrat's all-caps convention is very 2015 startup. Manrope feels contemporary without feeling trendy — it will remain relevant as the brand grows.

Brand Voice Persona — "Alex"

HomeDirectAI's voice is Alex — a 35-year-old Tampa Bay resident who has done multiple transactions, works in tech or finance, got tired of watching money leave at closings, and is now your knowledgeable friend who explains things clearly and pulls no punches.

Alex DOES say

04 — Brand Story Arc

The Hero's Journey —
the customer is Luke. We're Yoda.

Every homepage, ad, and email should map to this arc. The customer makes the bold choice. HomeDirectAI equips them to win. The villain is the broken commission system — not agents as individuals.

1
Act 1 · The World As It Is
The Commission System Was Built Against You
"Every year, millions of Americans hand $20,000–$50,000 to real estate agents at closing. Most never knew they were paying the buyer's agent's fee too. The system was built by agents, for agents — and the rules haven't changed in decades."
This is the "before state" — the world the customer lives in right now. The goal is to name the injustice clearly so the ICP feels seen and validated. 49% of sellers didn't know they paid the buyer's agent. 67% believe agents prioritize commissions over outcomes. Surface these facts without being preachy — let the numbers do the work.
Emotion: Righteous Indignation ICP trigger: Commission sticker shock Data: $23,400 on $430K home
2
Act 2 · The Call to Change
The Crack in the System — and the Third Option
"The NAR settlement cracked it open. AI made it possible. Now you don't have to choose between a 5.5% agent and a risky FSBO. There's a third option — and it's the one the industry didn't want you to find."
Introduce HomeDirectAI as the answer that the disruption created. The Sage archetype dominates here — showing the data, showing the math, proving the AI works. This is where the savings calculator lives, where AI negotiation case studies go, where the "how it works" process eliminates fear. Key objection to address: "This is too new for the biggest purchase of my life." Address with licensed credibility + AI track record + human escalation path.
Emotion: Hope + Curiosity Tool: Savings Calculator Trust: Licensed + AI-backed
3
Act 3 · The New World
You Closed. You Kept What You Earned.
"Buyers and sellers using HomeDirectAI keep their equity. AI negotiates with precision. A local chaperone shows the home. You close at 1%. You keep what you worked for."
The transformation state — the "after." This is where testimonials, real savings numbers, and named case studies live. Specificity is critical: "$18,200 saved by Sarah K., Westchase" converts. "Save thousands" does not. The chaperone model appears here as proof that human connection was never sacrificed — only the unnecessary, expensive middleman was removed.
Emotion: Pride + Financial Control Proof: Named testimonials Visual: Savings Gold numbers
→
The Call to Action
The customer becomes the hero — right now
The story doesn't end with inspiration. It ends with a specific, low-commitment action that delivers immediate value. The savings calculator is the primary entry point — it produces a personalized number that makes the value proposition undeniable before any signup is required.
Top of funnel → Calculator (no commitment) · Mid funnel → Start Sale or Valuation · Bottom → List My Home — 1%
